Task 1: A few or A little?

Fill in the gaps with "a few" (for countable) or "a little" (for uncountable).

1. I have friends in London, so I'm not lonely.
2. Could you give me help with this box?
3. We have minutes before the bus leaves.
4. She added sugar to her tea to make it sweeter.
5. I only know words in Spanish.
6. There is milk left in the fridge, enough for your coffee.

Task 2: Focus on Meaning

Fill in "few" or "little" (negative meaning - not enough).

1. The town was very quiet. There were people in the streets.
2. I'm sad because I have money left this month.
3. He is very shy. He has friends.
4. I'm sorry, I speak English, I can't help you.
5. We must hurry. There is time.

Task 3: Mixed Practice

Choose the best option: a little, little, a few, or few.

1. I'm not hungry. I just want soup.
2. Very students passed the exam; it was too difficult.
3. Enjoy your stay! We have interesting places to visit here.
4. The soup tastes bland. It needs salt.
5. He is lonely because he has people to talk to.
6. I have questions about the project. Can we talk?
